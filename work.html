Slack Team Optimization

Real-time team analytics and chemistry insights

crisis_alert Situation

Managers assemble teams by gut, with no objective read on whether people will work well together and no way to see it before the team is live. The client had a team-chemistry instrument validated through real-world participant testing, but no product that put it in a manager's hands inside the tools they already use.

lightbulb Decision

Built it Slack-native, where teams already work, rather than a standalone app to adopt. Capped the chemistry score at 88% because the validated data never produced scores in the 90s — fidelity to reality over flattering numbers. Built a descending-score guarantee so ranked team options never show confusing ties. Delivered a functional demo in six weeks on top of the validated instrument. Rejected: a separate app, an uncapped score that would mislead, and shipping a chemistry score that hadn't been validated.

shield Risk

The biggest risk wasn't technical — it was scope drift in a direction the underlying validation data couldn't defend. She held the build to what the science actually supported, even when the pressure was to ship more. She also designed against false confidence by anchoring scores to validation data rather than letting the model overstate.

check_circle Change (projected — investor-ready demo)

A manager can see team chemistry before committing to a configuration, compare clearly-ranked options, and act without psychology expertise — and the validation discipline makes the tool defensible to a buyer or investor, not merely slick.

Multi-Processor Billing System

Unified payment platform with Canadian compliance

crisis_alert Situation

A regulated Canadian professional-services firm needed billing simple enough for a non-technical solo practitioner to run, yet compliant with strict trust-accounting rules and Canadian data-residency law. The incumbent vendors were predatory, which is what prompted building an alternative. The real problem: make strict compliance invisible to the user while keeping an immutable audit trail.

lightbulb Decision

Unified three payment methods — card, pre-authorized debit, and Interac e-Transfer — behind one interface, because ~95% of the real users pay by Interac and avoid card fees; built for how they actually pay, not a card-first default. Built for scale from day one. Kept client data resident in Canada. Automated the tax split between professional fees and government disbursements. Rejected: a single-processor card-first design, a manual-first MVP ("build it to grow fast"), a cheaper money-transfer service that wasn't actually a processor, and convenient US-hosted defaults.

shield Risk

A trust-accounting error puts the user's professional standing in regulatory jeopardy. She designed around it with an immutable audit trail and automatic separation of held versus earned funds — so compliance is enforced by the system, not left to the user's diligence — and kept data in Canada to meet privacy law.

check_circle Change (projected — pre-launch, releasing soon)

For the firm adopting it: billing that's faster and safer to run, with compliance handled automatically rather than manually policed, and built to scale as the firm grows.

Hydrological Dashboard

Real-Time Flood Monitoring for Dauphin Lake, Manitoba

crisis_alert Situation

A 3,420 sq mi watershed drains through seven tributaries into shallow Dauphin Lake, feeding five beach communities, farmland, and a fishery. The 2011 flood (lake at 860.76 ft) is living memory, and 2026 opened with the highest snowpack in the record. The only official picture was provincial PDF outlooks and raw federal gauge data no resident reads. The problem wasn't building a dashboard — it was turning existing data into something a property owner could act on, in a season that looked like it might rival 2011.

lightbulb Decision

Trust authoritative instrument data and degrade gracefully — if the live API drops, show verified readings and point to the official source rather than break. Watch the leading indicator (seven tributaries, converted to a projected rate of change) instead of the lagging lake. Forecast in four probability bands tied to actions, and revise publicly as conditions change. Complement the province, never impersonate it. Hold the scope to monitoring and lock both domains. Rejected: single-source feeds, a lone lake-level readout, deterministic predictions, and "all things Dauphin Lake" scope.

shield Risk

Reliability through a storm's network stress (designed-in fallback); accuracy with honest uncertainty (probability bands, an explicit wind caveat, the not-official disclaimer); graduated action guidance so the tool neither cries wolf nor under-warns; zero-cost durability so it can't die from a hosting bill.

check_circle Change

A watershed with no independent monitor now has one place turning federal data into plain-language, action-tied guidance, refreshed every 15 minutes against the 2011/2013/2014 record. Through a record snowpack, the basin was handled and the lake never reached the most-likely peak band — and the tool made that calm legible in real time. Good judgment here looks like nothing happening; the tool made the "nothing happened" visible.

Gracious Signs

A custom sign shop's full migration off WordPress to a modern static stack — without losing a single SEO URL.

gracioussigns.com — live, fast, and editable by the client

crisis_alert

Situation

A local sign business's website was a fragile WordPress "Jenga tower" — a heavy theme, a page builder, a slider plugin, an unused store, and dozens of plugins stacked so that fixing one risked breaking another. The admin was painfully slow, mobile performance was poor, and months of SEO ranking were at constant risk from the next plugin update.

lightbulb

Decision

Stabilized the existing site first — a plugin audit and image work that alone lifted mobile PageSpeed from 38 to 55 and fixed layout shift. Then made the bigger call: kill WordPress entirely and rebuild on a static stack. Chose a Git-based CMS delivered as a CDN script rather than an installed dependency, so the live site has zero runtime dependence on it. Rejected: continuing to patch WordPress, and even a clean WordPress rebuild — the platform itself was the problem.

shield

Risk

The real risk was losing months of SEO at cutover — managed with 301 redirects for every changed URL, verbatim content on identical slugs, a resubmitted sitemap, and two weeks of daily monitoring. Security: WordPress is the most-attacked CMS, and removing the server, database, and PHP eliminated the attack surface. A non-technical client who still edits content: handled with a simpler CMS and a short training.

check_circle

Change

The site went from a seven-second admin and a fragile plugin stack to instant static loads, automatic modern image formats, and zero hosting cost — every SEO URL preserved. The client edits content without a developer, and the ongoing relationship shifts from "keep the site alive" to "grow the business."
